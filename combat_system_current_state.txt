Idle King Combat System - Current State
=====================================

This document describes the current implemented combat behavior without changing any code.


1. COMBAT SYSTEM
----------------

The current combat runtime uses a 3-layer structure:

1. CombatManager
2. CombatBridge
3. Character / GameWorld

CombatManager
- File: Scripts/Autoload/CombatManager.gd
- This is the authoritative combat logic layer.
- It owns:
  - king HP
  - enemy HP
  - king damage
  - enemy damage
  - king attack timing
  - enemy attack timing
  - wave spawning/progression
  - post-hit pacing delay
  - combat entry delay
- It does not play animations directly.
- It emits combat events such as:
  - king_attack_performed
  - enemy_attack_performed
  - combat_state_changed
  - wave_started
  - wave_changed

CombatBridge
- File: Scripts/Battle/CombatBridge.gd
- This is the synchronization layer between manager logic and battle visuals.
- It:
  - listens to attack signals from CombatManager
  - triggers attack animations on the active hero/enemy
  - listens for attack_hit from Character
  - resolves damage through CombatManager
  - updates visual HP after damage resolution
  - applies momentum bias to hit/recoil presentation
- It does not own damage authority.

Character
- File: Scripts/Battle/Character.gd
- This is the visual and movement layer.
- It owns:
  - sprite-sheet animation playback
  - facing
  - movement
  - knockback/recoil motion
  - hit flash
  - target chasing / spacing behavior
- It does not calculate battle damage.
- It emits attack_hit at the attack impact point.

GameWorld
- File: Scripts/Battle/GameWorld.gd
- Hosts the battle scene inside the SubViewport.
- Spawns hero and enemy visuals.
- Connects the bridge to the current combatants.
- Provides feedback effects:
  - damage numbers
  - camera shake
  - hit-stop

General flow
- CombatManager opens attack windows and emits an attack request.
- CombatBridge tells the correct Character to play its attack animation.
- Character reaches the attack impact frame and emits attack_hit.
- CombatBridge calls resolve_king_attack() or resolve_enemy_attack() on CombatManager.
- CombatManager applies the real damage.
- CombatBridge syncs the target's visual HP and triggers hit feedback.


2. COMBAT JUICE
---------------

Current combat juice is split between Character.gd and GameWorld.gd.

Present feedback systems
- Hit-stop
  - File: Scripts/Battle/GameWorld.gd
  - Uses Engine.time_scale = 0.05 briefly, then restores it.
  - Current duration is short but noticeable.

- Camera shake
  - File: Scripts/Battle/GameWorld.gd
  - Applies a random camera offset on hit, then resets after a short delay.

- Floating damage numbers
  - File: Scripts/Battle/GameWorld.gd
  - Spawns a Label at the hit position.
  - Tweens it upward and fades it out.

- Hit flash
  - File: Scripts/Battle/Character.gd
  - Temporarily tints the sprite brighter on hit, then returns to white.

- Post-hit pacing
  - File: Scripts/Autoload/CombatManager.gd
  - Uses POST_HIT_PACING_DELAY so attacks do not immediately chain with no rhythm.

Current feel goal
- Hits are meant to feel punchy and readable rather than purely statistical.
- The game currently uses:
  - visual pause
  - shake
  - flash
  - floating numbers
  - short rhythm lock after contact

Current limitation
- The combat feel is present, but its readability still depends heavily on how visible knockback/recoil is during real gameplay.


3. KNOCKBACK SYSTEM
-------------------

Files involved
- Scripts/Battle/Character.gd
- Scripts/Battle/CombatBridge.gd

Current design
- Knockback is visual-only.
- CombatBridge decides who got hit and who should recoil after damage resolves.
- Character applies the actual motion force locally.

Current hit reaction path
- When the king lands a hit:
  - CombatBridge resolves king damage through CombatManager.
  - Enemy receives apply_hit(...)
  - Hero receives apply_recoil(...)

- When the enemy lands a hit:
  - CombatBridge resolves enemy damage through CombatManager.
  - Hero receives apply_hit(...)
  - Enemy receives apply_recoil(...)

Force model
- Character.gd currently uses:
  - _knockback_velocity_x
  - _knockback_time_remaining
- Knockback temporarily overrides normal AI movement.
- During knockback:
  - the character enters a short forced reaction phase
  - velocity decays smoothly over time

Variation
- Knockback is not fully constant.
- Force currently varies through:
  - base force floor
  - max force cap
  - damage-based scaling
  - randomized variation multiplier
  - edge resistance near world bounds

Current balancing helpers
- Edge resistance:
  - reduces knockback when already near the stage boundary
  - prevents repeated pushes from pinning one unit to the side too easily

- Soft center bias:
  - gently encourages re-centering during close combat
  - avoids permanent long-term drift

- Momentum system:
  - File: Scripts/Battle/CombatBridge.gd
  - Momentum ranges toward one side when that side lands hits
  - It biases:
    - target knockback
    - attacker recoil reduction
  - It also decays over time to avoid permanent dominance

Important distinction
- apply_hit(...) is the stronger hit reaction
- apply_recoil(...) is the smaller recoil on the attacker

Current limitation
- The intended system is symmetric, but the visible result still depends on live tuning of:
  - force strength
  - decay
  - friction
  - attack range braking
  - momentum bias


4. COMBAT DAMAGE SYSTEM
-----------------------

Authoritative damage owner
- CombatManager.gd is the only real damage authority.

What CombatManager controls
- king_damage
- enemy_damage
- king_current_hp
- enemy_current_hp
- attack cooldown readiness
- attack lock timers
- wave-based enemy stat generation

How damage is applied
- The bridge never directly subtracts HP.
- Character never directly calculates battle damage.
- Only CombatManager does that, inside:
  - resolve_king_attack()
  - resolve_enemy_attack()

King attack path
- King attack timer arms a king attack.
- When the king is in an open attack window and not locked:
  - king_attack_performed is emitted
- Character plays the animation
- At impact frame:
  - attack_hit is emitted
- CombatBridge calls resolve_king_attack()
- CombatManager subtracts HP from enemy_current_hp

Enemy attack path
- Enemy cooldown timer re-arms enemy attack readiness.
- If enemy is in a valid attack window:
  - enemy_attack_performed is emitted
- Character plays the animation
- At impact frame:
  - attack_hit is emitted
- CombatBridge calls resolve_enemy_attack()
- CombatManager subtracts HP from king_current_hp

Wave scaling
- File: Scripts/Autoload/CombatManager.gd
- Enemy data is created by _create_enemy_data(wave)
- Normal waves currently scale:
  - HP
  - damage
  - reward gold
  - attack cooldown
- Boss waves use a separate stat curve and slower/heavier cadence.

Current king progression
- king_damage is driven by upgrades
- king attack interval is driven by upgrades
- king HP scales with wave through _refresh_king_health_pool()


5. SUMMARY
----------

Current implemented state:
- Combat is real-time and autonomous
- CombatManager is authoritative for damage and timing
- CombatBridge synchronizes visual attacks and hit resolution
- Character handles movement, spacing, knockback, recoil, and animations
- GameWorld adds presentation feedback like hit-stop, shake, and damage numbers

Current practical feel:
- The system already has:
  - impact timing
  - hit-stop
  - shake
  - flash
  - recoil
  - floating damage numbers
  - wave-based combat math

Current main tuning sensitivity:
- The battle feel is now mostly a tuning problem, not an architecture problem.
- The most sensitive values are:
  - knockback force
  - recoil force
  - momentum gain/decay
  - attack pacing delay
  - movement friction around attack range
