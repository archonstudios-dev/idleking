Combat Detailed Audit
=====================

Purpose
-------

This file documents the current live combat behavior as it exists in the project right now.

It is meant to answer:
- how combat is actually structured
- where attack timing comes from
- why hits can look desynced even when damage resolves correctly
- why attack animation variety is limited


1. COMBAT ARCHITECTURE
----------------------

The combat system uses 4 main layers:

1. `CombatManager`
- authoritative gameplay logic
- owns HP, damage, timers, wave progression, defeat handling
- decides when an attack is allowed
- decides when damage is actually applied

2. `CombatBridge`
- sync layer between manager and character visuals
- receives attack events from manager
- tells characters to play attack animations
- resolves hit visuals after `attack_hit`
- applies defender hit reaction and attacker recoil

3. `Character`
- visual/motion layer only
- plays animations
- moves toward target
- emits `attack_hit` at the configured impact time
- handles recoil, knockback, hit stun, death visuals

4. `GameWorld`
- spawns hero/enemy visuals
- binds `CombatBridge`
- provides hit-stop, shake, damage numbers, and hit sound


2. AUTHORITATIVE VS VISUAL RESPONSIBILITIES
-------------------------------------------

Authoritative systems:
- HP values
- damage values
- wave progression
- attack cooldowns
- defeat conditions

Visual-only systems:
- attack animation playback
- hit flash
- knockback and recoil motion
- hit-stop
- camera shake
- floating damage numbers

Important consequence:
- a hit can be mathematically correct even if the visual contact feels wrong
- damage authority and visual impact timing are separate systems


3. CURRENT ATTACK FLOW
----------------------

King side:
1. `CombatManager` decides the king can attack
2. `king_attack_performed` is emitted
3. `CombatBridge._on_king_attack_requested()` calls `hero.play_attack_animation()`
4. `Character` starts the attack animation
5. At the animation impact point, `Character.attack_hit` is emitted
6. `CombatBridge._on_hero_attack_hit()` calls `CombatManager.resolve_king_attack()`
7. Manager subtracts enemy HP
8. Bridge applies:
   - defender hit reaction
   - hit feedback in `GameWorld`
   - attacker recoil

Enemy side:
1. `CombatManager` decides the enemy can attack
2. `enemy_attack_performed` is emitted
3. `CombatBridge._on_enemy_attack_requested()` calls `enemy.play_attack_animation()`
4. `Character` starts the enemy attack animation
5. At the enemy impact point, `Character.attack_hit` is emitted
6. `CombatBridge._on_enemy_attack_hit()` calls `CombatManager.resolve_enemy_attack()`
7. Manager subtracts king HP
8. Bridge applies:
   - defender hit reaction
   - hit feedback in `GameWorld`
   - attacker recoil

5. HOW ATTACK CONTACT IS CURRENTLY DETERMINED
---------------------------------------------

Actual damage is not applied when the attack animation starts.

Damage is applied only when:
- the attacking `Character` reaches its impact time
- `attack_hit` is emitted
- bridge calls manager resolution

That means:
- animation start and hit confirmation are separate
- if the impact frame does not line up with the sprite contact pose, the hit looks wrong even though the manager is correct


6. WHY HITS CAN LOOK LIKE THEY ARE NOT ACTUALLY HITTING
-------------------------------------------------------

There are several current reasons:

1. Animation contact pose vs impact time are not artist-authored
- The system uses a ratio-based timing model.
- Hero and enemy impact frames are estimated by ratio, not by explicit contact notifies.
- If the sprite pack's real contact frame differs from the ratio, the hit appears early or late.

2. Defender reaction and attacker recoil are separate events
- The bridge first applies defender hit.
- Then attacker recoil is applied after a short deferred step.
- This is cleaner than same-frame reaction, but if the defender pose already looks disconnected, it can still feel staged.

3. Hit-stop is global, not character-authored
- `GameWorld` freezes the whole world with `Engine.time_scale`.
- This creates impact emphasis, but it is not tied to specific limbs/poses.
- If the freeze lands slightly off the ideal contact pose, it highlights the desync instead of hiding it.

4. Shared attack windows use melee contact buffering
- `CombatManager.set_attack_windows()` keeps attack windows open while either side is in shared contact.
- This improves fluidity, but can also allow an attack request to visually continue even when spacing looks borderline.

5. Visual knockback is not the same thing as hit confirmation
- Damage is authoritative.
- Knockback is presentation force.
- So a hit can deal damage while the motion response still feels too weak, too late, or too floaty.


7. WHY KNOCKBACK CAN LOOK DESYNCED
---------------------------------

Current knockback/recoil behavior:
- defender hit reaction uses `apply_hit(...)`
- attacker uses `apply_recoil(...)`
- both call `_apply_directional_force(...)`
- force is damage-relative, then modified by:
  - variance
  - resistance
  - edge resistance
  - bridge bias

Why it can still read poorly:

1. Hit-stop and motion are layered separately
- hit-stop occurs in `GameWorld`
- knockback is applied in `Character`
- bridge reaction order tries to help, but the feel still depends on whether the stop, flash, and motion align on the right frame

2. Recoil is lighter than defender hit
- `RECOIL_FORCE_FACTOR = 0.72`
- attacker response is intentionally softer
- this is good for readability, but can also look like the attacker is "sliding" instead of committing

3. Lane correction and tether behavior can swallow pushback
- `SOFT_TETHER_PULL`
- `SOFT_CENTER_PULL`
- `LANE_CORRECTION_DELAY`
- `REENGAGE_BOOST_TIME`
- these systems help keep fighters together
- but they also reduce how long knockback stays visually readable

4. Edge resistance changes the same hit visually
- near stage edges, knockback is reduced
- identical hits can look different depending on world position


8. WHY THE KING CAN LOOK LIKE HE IS GETTING DOUBLE HIT
------------------------------------------------------

This feeling can come from stacked visual layers:

1. The king takes the defender hit reaction
- `apply_hit(...)`

2. Global hit-stop triggers on the same exchange
- world freeze emphasizes the contact

3. Camera shake and floating damage number trigger too
- these are both instant feedback layers

4. If enemy animation contact is slightly late or early
- the visual sequence can feel like:
  - spell/weapon pose
  - freeze
  - king reacts
  - recoil/settle still finishing

This can read like "two hits" even when only one manager-side damage event happened.


9. CURRENT CHARACTER ANIMATION LIMITATIONS
------------------------------------------

Current reality:
- hero attack animation sequence = one entry
- enemy attack animation sequence = one entry

Specifically:
- `Character._attack_sequence = ["attack"]`
- there are no alternating combo entries
- there are no light/heavy variants
- there are no context-sensitive attack variants

Result:
- both sides currently use one attack animation repeatedly
- combat can become visually repetitive even if the stats and timing differ

11. CURRENT FACING / ORIENTATION RULES
--------------------------------------

Facing is target-driven during:
- chase
- attack
- knockback recovery

Recent important fix:

- they now keep trying to face their target

This solved the visible "wrong direction for one frame" bug, but it does not solve contact timing by itself.


12. CURRENT SPACING AND RANGE RULES
-----------------------------------

Character range logic:
- `attack_range`
- `hitbox_range`
- `HITBOX_Y_TOLERANCE`

Current result:
- fighters can be in visually close contact while still relying on ratio-based hit timing
- if the sprite contact frame does not line up with range satisfaction, the exchange looks soft or fake


13. WHAT IS CURRENTLY WORKING
-----------------------------

- authoritative damage resolution is working
- wave progression is working
- persistent enemy encounters are working
- attack animations do trigger from manager events
- hit-stop, shake, flash, and damage numbers all work
- defeat flow works
- knockback exists on both sides


14. WHAT CURRENTLY FEELS WEAK
-----------------------------

- contact timing is not fully believable
- knockback can read detached from the exact hit moment
- one attack animation causes repetition
- recoil and defender hit do not always read as one coherent exchange
- current presentation layers are active, but not yet fully synchronized to the strongest contact frame


15. MOST LIKELY REASONS COMBAT FEELS "DESYNCED"
-----------------------------------------------

Based on the live code, the biggest reasons are:

1. Impact timing is estimated, not hand-authored per animation
2. Only one attack animation is used, so repetition makes timing flaws easier to notice
3. Defender hit, attacker recoil, hit-stop, flash, shake, and damage numbers are all separate layers that must line up
4. Tether/centering systems reduce the visual persistence of knockback
5. The system is mechanically correct, but not yet presentation-locked for release-level feel


16. PRACTICAL SUMMARY
---------------------

Current combat is:
- structurally solid
- manager-authoritative
- visually functional
- not yet fully synchronized at the presentation layer

The current project does NOT have:
- broken damage authority
- missing hit resolution
- missing attack triggers

The current project DOES have:
- ratio-based contact timing
- visible separation between mathematical hit and visual hit
- repetitive attack presentation
- knockback/readability issues caused by multiple timing layers interacting

Short version:
- the characters are fighting correctly in system logic
- but the presentation layer still does not always sell the hit as a single believable contact event
- animation variety is currently minimal because only one attack animation is active per side

19. WHY THE CHARACTERS LOOK LIKE THEY ARE FIGHTING BUT NOT CONNECTING
---------------------------------------------------------------------

This is the core readability problem in plain language:

1. The attack animation starts
- this visually tells the player "a strike is beginning"

2. The actual hit happens later based on a ratio
- not based on an explicit hand-picked frame event

3. The defender reaction, shake, flash, freeze, number pop, and recoil all stack together
- these are correct systems
- but they do not always align with the strongest visual contact pose

4. The result is:
- the fight is logically correct
- but the eye does not always read the hit as a clean collision

That creates the exact feeling of:
- "they are fighting"
but also
- "it doesn't look like the hit really landed"


20. WHY KNOCKBACK FEELS OUT OF SYNC SPECIFICALLY
------------------------------------------------

Current order in presentation:
- attack animation reaches impact point
- `attack_hit` emits
- manager resolves damage
- bridge applies defender hit response
- `GameWorld` applies:
  - damage number
  - shake
  - hit-stop
  - sound
- attacker recoil also happens as part of bridge response

Why this can still feel wrong:
- all these feedback layers are technically tied to the hit
- but if the sprite pose is not the perfect contact pose at that moment, the feedback highlights the mismatch

So knockback feels desynced not because the code is random, but because:
- contact timing
- sprite pose
- reaction order
- tether recentering

are all individually reasonable, but not yet fully harmonized into one clean visual beat


21. WHY THE CURRENT SYSTEM DOES NOT YET FEEL RELEASE-READY
----------------------------------------------------------

The system is mechanically solid, but release feel usually requires:
- exact contact readability
- low ambiguity on when a hit lands
- visible difference between attack variants
- reactions that look authored, not estimated

Current live weaknesses that block that feeling:
- one repeated attack animation per side
- ratio-timed impacts instead of animation-authored contact points
- knockback readability being shortened by lane/tether correction
- multiple feedback systems landing together without perfectly matching the strongest contact frame


22. WHAT A GOOD NEXT COMBAT PASS SHOULD TARGET
----------------------------------------------

Without redesigning architecture, the next pass should focus on:
- attack animation variety
- exact per-animation impact timing
- cleaner one-beat synchronization of:
  - hit confirmation
  - defender reaction
  - attacker recoil
  - hit-stop
  - shake
  - damage number
- slightly longer readable separation after impact

That is the path from:
- "combat works"
to
- "combat looks like real contact"


23. FINAL DIAGNOSIS
-------------------

Current truth:
- combat logic is working
- attacks do resolve
- knockback does exist
- hit feedback does trigger

But the presentation still reads weak because:
- attack variety is low
- contact timing is estimated
- feedback layers are not yet fully authored to the same exact visual beat

Short version:
- the combat problem is no longer system authority

25. EXACT LIVE ENEMY-HIT FLOW

   - `attack_hit.emit(self)` fires

   - resolves damage in `CombatManager`

   - applies king hit reaction
   - applies enemy recoil
   - triggers hit feedback in `GameWorld`

Important finding:
- the king reaction itself is not deferred anymore
- it is applied immediately inside `_on_enemy_attack_hit()`

That means the "late" feeling is not coming from a remaining explicit wait in `CombatBridge`.

29. WHY THE KING LOOKS DELAYED SPECIFICALLY ON ENEMY ATTACKS
------------------------------------------------------------

Based on the current live code, the most likely exact reason is:

1. Enemy impact timing is early for its longer 8-frame attacks
- current enemy hit times are around `0.320s` and `0.367s`
- if the real contact pose is later, the king reaction appears out of sync

2. Enemy attacks now happen from a wider allowed spacing
- `attack_range = 70`
- `hitbox_range = 74`

3. Hit-stop still freezes immediately on the same event
- reaction is immediate in code
- but the freeze emphasizes whatever pose the enemy is in on the impact frame
- if that pose is not the strongest contact pose, the king can still look "late"

4. The problem is asymmetric because the enemy sheets are longer
- the hero's 4-frame attacks hide timing drift better
- the enemy's 8-frame attacks expose timing drift more

Most important conclusion:
- the current late-feeling reaction is primarily an enemy impact-timing problem, not a king reaction-delay problem

33. FOLLOW-UP AUDIT: WHY THE KING CAN LOOK KNOCKED BACK TWICE
-------------------------------------------------------------

Current observed symptom:
- one enemy hit lands
- king HP only changes once
- but the king can visually read like he gets pushed twice

This is a different problem from "damage twice".
It is a presentation overlap problem.


34. WHAT THE LIVE CODE PROVES
-----------------------------

The king is not taking two authoritative hits from one enemy strike.

Why:
- `CombatManager.resolve_enemy_attack()` only subtracts HP once per pending enemy attack
- `_enemy_attack_pending` is cleared immediately inside `resolve_enemy_attack()`
- `king_damaged.emit(...)` only fires once for that resolved attack
- there is no second manager-side damage path connected to the king visual

So the "double hit" feeling is visual only, not gameplay logic.


35. MOST LIKELY EXACT VISUAL CAUSE
----------------------------------

The strongest remaining suspect in the live code is:

- the king's own attack/recoil state can overlap with the enemy hit reaction state

Current reason:
- on king attacks, `CombatBridge._on_hero_attack_hit()` applies:
  - enemy defender hit
  - king attacker recoil

- on enemy attacks, `CombatBridge._on_enemy_attack_hit()` applies:
  - king defender hit
  - enemy attacker recoil

If the king is in or near his own attack follow-through when the enemy hit lands, the eye can read:
- king settles/recoils from his own action
- then king also gets enemy hit reaction

That feels like:
- "one hit, two backward motions"

even though only one enemy damage event happened.


36. WHY THIS SHOWS UP MOSTLY WHEN THE ENEMY ATTACKS
---------------------------------------------------

Because the king is the one receiving both of these visual layers:

1. His own attacker-side motion from earlier exchange timing
2. The enemy-hit defender reaction

The enemy does not expose the issue as strongly because:
- the enemy's presentation is less readable than the king's
- the player's eye is focused on the king's body language

So the problem is not just:
- enemy impact timing

It is now more specifically:
- enemy impact timing plus king carry-over motion overlap


37. SPECIFIC LIVE CODE PATH THAT ALLOWS THE OVERLAP
---------------------------------------------------

In `Character.apply_hit(...)`:
- `_apply_directional_force(...)` happens immediately
- recovery is entered immediately

But current hit interruption logic is conditional:

- if `_is_attacking`
  - and `profile == "hero"`
  - and `not _attack_hit_emitted`
    - break the attack immediately
  - else
    - `_queued_attack = true`
    - `return`

That means:
- if the king has already emitted his own attack hit
- but is still visually in attack follow-through
- then enemy hit can arrive
- and the function may return early without forcing the full hit animation takeover

Result:
- knockback force is already applied
- but the visual state can still be partially carrying attack/follow-through
- then the actual body read of the hit feels split into two beats

This is the most likely exact source of the "double knockback" feeling.


38. SECONDARY CONTRIBUTING FACTOR
---------------------------------

Current hit-stop still happens after reactions are applied:
- bridge applies reactions
- `GameWorld.apply_hit_feedback()` triggers hit-stop

This is good for immediacy, but it can still preserve a half-transition pose if:
- attack follow-through is not fully canceled
- then the freeze catches the king between attack motion and hit motion

That can visually exaggerate the impression of:
- "first he moved from his own attack"
- "then he got knocked again"


39. WHAT THE NEXT FIX SHOULD TARGET
-----------------------------------

To solve this specific issue, the next fix should target:

- king hit interruption after enemy hit

More specifically:
- when the king is the defender and gets hit by the enemy,
  force the king fully out of attack/follow-through visual state
  even if `_attack_hit_emitted` is already true

That means:
- no conditional partial carry-over on king defender hit
- the enemy hit should fully own the king's visual state on that beat

Short version:
- the king likely is not reacting late anymore
- he is visually carrying over too much of his own action before the enemy hit fully takes over


40. CURRENT BEST DIAGNOSIS
--------------------------

The remaining problem is most likely:
- not double damage
- not a second knockback force from gameplay logic
- but overlapping king motion layers

Most likely exact source:
- king attack follow-through is not always fully canceled when enemy hit lands

That is why one enemy hit can still read like:
- first backward motion
- second backward motion

even though the combat system only resolved one real hit.
