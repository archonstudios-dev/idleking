Current Version Notes
=====================

This file explains the current live combat behavior without changing any gameplay code.


1. KNOCKBACK AND DAMAGE SYSTEM
------------------------------

Current architecture:
- CombatManager is the only real damage authority.
- CombatBridge resolves hits after animation impact.
- Character only handles visual motion, hit reaction, and animation.

Current hit flow:
1. CombatManager emits `king_attack_performed` or `enemy_attack_performed`.
2. CombatBridge tells the attacker Character to play its attack animation.
3. Character emits `attack_hit` at about 45% of the animation.
4. CombatBridge calls:
   - `resolve_king_attack()`
   - `resolve_enemy_attack()`
5. CombatManager subtracts HP.
6. CombatBridge then applies visual reactions:
   - defender gets `apply_hit(...)`
   - attacker gets `apply_recoil(...)`

So:
- damage is authoritative and manager-driven
- knockback is visual-only and bridge/character-driven


2. DO THE CHARACTERS HAVE KNOCKBACK?
------------------------------------

Yes.

Both characters currently have:
- defender knockback through `apply_hit(...)`
- attacker recoil through `apply_recoil(...)`

Files involved:
- `Scripts/Battle/CombatBridge.gd`
- `Scripts/Battle/Character.gd`

Important detail:
- both sides are supposed to react on every landed hit
- the defender reaction is stronger
- the attacker recoil is smaller


3. WHY ENEMY KNOCKBACK IS NOT AS VISUALLY OBVIOUS
-------------------------------------------------

The enemy can still appear to have weaker knockback even when the same system is being called.

Main reasons in the current version:

1. The enemy often has more damage momentum working in its favor
- `CombatBridge.gd` keeps a momentum value.
- When one side keeps landing hits, momentum biases the defender push and reduces attacker recoil.
- If the enemy is controlling exchanges, the king gets shoved more and the enemy recoils less.

2. The enemy may visually recover faster because of lane behavior
- `Character.gd` uses:
  - tether pull
  - center pull
  - attack-range braking
  - re-engage boost
- These systems can visually hide some enemy pushback because the enemy is pulled back into the lane quickly.

3. Recoil is smaller than direct knockback
- The attacker reaction uses `RECOIL_FORCE_FACTOR = 0.72`.
- So even when both units react, the attacker side will look lighter than the defender side.

4. Edge resistance changes what is visible
- Knockback is reduced near the stage boundary.
- If the enemy is closer to a safer zone or gets pulled back faster, its reaction can feel less dramatic.

Short version:
- enemy knockback exists
- it is just less readable because current momentum, recoil scaling, and re-centering behavior reduce how much of it stays visible


4. IS KNOCKBACK RELATIVE TO DAMAGE?
-----------------------------------

Yes.

Current code:
- `Character._apply_directional_force(...)`

Knockback strength depends on:
- incoming damage amount
- force multiplier from bridge bias
- momentum bias
- HP-based resistance
- edge resistance
- small random variation

The current force formula is effectively:
- higher damage -> stronger force
- but still clamped between a minimum and maximum

Important modifiers:
- `DAMAGE_FORCE_SCALE = 0.95`
- `MIN_IMPACT_FORCE = 26.0`
- `MAX_IMPACT_FORCE = 72.0`
- `LOW_HP_KNOCKBACK_RESISTANCE = 0.72`

That means knockback is damage-relative, but not purely linear:
- it has a floor
- it has a cap
- it gets reduced by low-HP resistance
- it gets reduced near edges
- it gets shaped by bridge momentum


5. DAMAGE SYSTEM
----------------

Damage is not estimated visually.
It is directly controlled by `Scripts/Autoload/CombatManager.gd`.

King damage:
- comes from upgrades
- stored in `king_damage`
- updated by:
  - `UpgradeManager.get_combat_damage()`
  - `_apply_upgrade_stats(...)`

Enemy damage:
- comes from wave scaling
- stored in `enemy_damage`
- created in `_create_enemy_data(wave)`

Current enemy formulas:

Normal enemy:
- `damage = 2 + int(wave * 0.08)`

Boss enemy:
- `damage = 8 + int(wave * 0.22)`

So if you are seeing:
- king damage = 25
- enemy damage = 50

That is believable in the current build.

Why:
- king damage is probably your current upgrade value
- enemy damage is probably coming from a very high wave

Example:
- at wave 612, normal enemy damage becomes:
  - `2 + int(612 * 0.08) = 50`

So the system currently allows:
- king damage to be upgrade-limited
- enemy damage to continue scaling with wave

That is why combat can feel enemy-favored at very high waves.


6. SUGGESTIONS TO IMPROVE THESE SYSTEMS
--------------------------------------

Best improvements for the current version:

1. Reduce momentum snowball
- lower momentum gain per hit
- increase momentum decay
- cap momentum effect harder

Why:
- this would reduce the "winner keeps winning" feel

2. Bring defender knockback and attacker recoil closer together
- slightly reduce defender push
- slightly increase attacker recoil

Why:
- this improves the tug-of-war look
- it makes both units visibly react every exchange

3. Make enemy knockback stay visible for a little longer
- slightly increase knockback override time
- slightly reduce immediate lane correction right after enemy gets hit

Why:
- enemy pushback currently gets visually swallowed too fast

4. Add wave-side damage normalization
- either slow enemy damage scaling at high waves
- or let king damage scale harder through upgrades/resources

Why:
- the current `25 vs 50` type split can be mathematically valid but still feel unfair

5. Separate "combat balance" from "presentation force"
- keep manager damage as-is if desired
- but decouple visual knockback force from raw damage above a threshold

Why:
- very high damage values can make reactions too predictable or too punishing
- this keeps fights readable without flattening combat math

6. Add defender-specific visibility boost
- when enemy gets hit, slightly reduce its center pull for a short window

Why:
- this would make enemy knockback easier to see without changing damage authority

7. Tune high-wave pacing
- if enemy damage rises faster than king damage, also slow enemy cadence or increase enemy recoil

Why:
- damage imbalance plus fast cadence makes one-sided lockouts more likely


7. PRACTICAL SUMMARY
--------------------

Current state:
- both characters do have knockback
- knockback is influenced by damage
- damage itself is fully controlled by CombatManager
- enemy knockback is less visible because the current visual balancing favors faster enemy recovery and momentum-biased exchanges

Most likely cause of what you are feeling:
- not that enemy has no knockback
- but that current momentum + recoil scaling + wave damage scaling makes the enemy look more stable and the king look more displaced

If this system is tuned next, the highest-value changes are:
- reduce momentum snowball
- increase visible enemy recoil/knockback persistence
- normalize very high-wave enemy damage relative to king progression


8. CURRENT GAMEPLAY LOOP
------------------------

The current gameplay loop is an automated wave-defense idle loop with visible battle presentation.

At a high level, the loop is:
1. The game spawns the current wave enemy.
2. Hero and enemy enter the stage.
3. There is a short combat entry delay.
4. Both units walk toward each other.
5. CombatManager opens the attack window once they are in melee contact.
6. Both sides auto-attack based on cooldown/timing.
7. Damage is resolved through CombatManager.
8. If the enemy dies, the wave advances and a new enemy is prepared.
9. If the king dies, the same wave is restored and replayed.

This is the current live sequence in more detail:

Combat start:
- `CombatManager._spawn_enemy_for_wave(wave)` sets the enemy stats.
- It also refreshes the king HP pool.
- It starts a short `COMBAT_ENTRY_DELAY`.
- During that delay:
  - attack windows are closed
  - `GameWorld` keeps both fighters unable to move
- When the delay ends:
  - `combat_entry_delay_changed(false)` is emitted
  - `GameWorld` enables movement
  - both fighters move toward each other

Engagement:
- `CombatBridge._physics_process()` checks whether hero and enemy are in range.
- It passes that to `CombatManager.set_attack_windows(...)`.
- `CombatManager` uses a shared melee-contact window with a small buffer.

Attack cycle:
- King:
  - `KingAttackTimer` sets `_king_attack_ready = true`
  - when in-range and unlocked:
    - `king_attack_performed` is emitted
- Enemy:
  - enemy cooldown timer sets `_enemy_attack_ready = true`
  - when in-range and unlocked:
    - `enemy_attack_performed` is emitted

Visual attack:
- `CombatBridge` receives the attack signal.
- It tells the right `Character` to play the attack animation.
- `Character` emits `attack_hit` around 45% of the animation.

Damage resolve:
- `CombatBridge` calls:
  - `resolve_king_attack()`
  - `resolve_enemy_attack()`
- `CombatManager` applies the real HP subtraction.
- `CombatBridge` then:
  - syncs target HP visually
  - applies knockback/recoil
  - triggers hit feedback in `GameWorld`

This means the current gameplay loop is:
- wait
- approach
- auto-fight
- resolve hit
- either advance wave or replay wave


9. WHAT HAPPENS WHEN THE KING HP REACHES 0
------------------------------------------

Current manager-side behavior:
- `CombatManager.resolve_enemy_attack()` subtracts HP from `king_current_hp`
- if HP reaches 0:
  - `king_damaged` is emitted
  - `combat_state_changed` is emitted
  - `king_defeated` is emitted

Current visual-side behavior:
- `GameWorld` receives the new combat state
- hero HP UI is updated to 0 through `hero.sync_health(...)`

Important current limitation:
- `sync_health()` only updates the health number
- it does not call `Character._die()`
- so the hero visual does not automatically enter the death animation when manager HP reaches 0

Why the king currently does not reliably play a death animation at 0 HP:
- the real HP is owned by `CombatManager`
- but the visual death state is owned by `Character`
- there is currently no explicit bridge from:
  - manager HP hit 0
  - into
  - `Character._die()` / visual death state

So the current version has a gap:
- manager knows the king is dead
- UI knows the king HP is 0
- but the hero Character is not always told to visually die

This is why the king can appear to hit 0 HP without a proper death animation.

Current practical result:
- the defeat state is logically recognized
- but the visual death presentation is incomplete / inconsistent


10. WHAT HAPPENS WHEN ENEMY HP REACHES 0
----------------------------------------

Current manager-side behavior:
- `CombatManager.resolve_king_attack()` subtracts HP from `enemy_current_hp`
- if HP reaches 0:
  - `_handle_enemy_defeat()` is called
  - gold is granted immediately
  - `enemy_defeated` is emitted
  - `current_wave` increments
  - next wave is stored in `_pending_wave_spawn`
  - enemy HP is set to 0
  - combat state is emitted

Current intended visual flow:
- the current enemy should finish a death presentation
- then `CombatBridge._on_enemy_died(...)` should call:
  - `spawn_pending_wave_enemy()`
  - `GameWorld.refresh_from_combat_state(...)`

But the current live limitation is similar to the king:
- the enemy visual HP is synced to 0 using `sync_health(...)`
- `sync_health()` does not automatically call `_die()`

So right now:
- the manager definitely knows the enemy is dead
- rewards and wave progression do happen manager-side
- but the enemy death animation is not guaranteed by the current sync path alone

Short version:
- logically, yes, enemy death is recognized
- visually, enemy death animation is not fully guaranteed by the current manager-to-character link

So the current answer is:
- enemy death animation is intended
- but in the current implementation it is not as robustly driven as it should be


11. WHY DEATH ANIMATIONS ARE CURRENTLY INCONSISTENT
---------------------------------------------------

The root issue is a split between:
- authoritative HP in `CombatManager`
- visual death state in `Character`

Right now:
- `Character._die()` is only guaranteed when local character-side damage code triggers it
- but the main live combat path no longer uses local character-side damage as the authority
- it uses manager-side damage + visual HP syncing

That means:
- HP goes to 0 in the manager
- but Character is not necessarily told:
  - "enter death state now"
  - "play death animation now"
  - "emit died when the death hold is done"

That is the current reason death animation behavior is weaker than the rest of the combat flow.


12. REPLAYABILITY: HOW THE CURRENT LOOP SUPPORTS IT
---------------------------------------------------

The game already has a basic replayable structure:

1. Infinite or long-form wave progression
- waves continue to increase
- enemy stats continue to scale
- boss waves appear periodically

2. Resource reward loop
- enemy defeat grants gold
- gold can feed upgrades
- upgrades feed combat strength

3. Passive automation loop
- the player does not manually control the battle
- progression comes from preparation, upgrades, and tuning

4. Reset/retry loop
- when the king loses, the current wave can be replayed

This means the project already has the skeleton of a replayable idle battler:
- push wave
- earn reward
- improve stats
- push farther


13. WHERE THE CURRENT LOOP IS STRONG
------------------------------------

Strong current foundations:
- clear 3-layer architecture
- deterministic damage authority
- visible battle instead of invisible timers
- scalable wave-based enemy generation
- gold reward progression
- upgrade-driven king growth
- boss cadence every 10 waves

These are good foundations for replayability because they create:
- measurable growth
- repeatable challenge
- readable progression milestones


14. WHERE THE CURRENT LOOP IS WEAK FOR REPLAYABILITY
----------------------------------------------------

Main weak spots in the current version:

1. Death presentation is not fully reliable
- losing a fight should feel like a clear event
- right now king/enemy death visuals are weaker than they should be

2. Wave scaling can outpace king growth
- enemy damage can become much higher than king damage at high waves
- this can make progress feel mathematically unfair instead of tactically difficult

3. Combat outcomes can feel too predetermined
- if momentum and damage scaling snowball too hard
- the battle can feel decided early

4. The replay loop is mostly numerical right now
- the player improves stats
- but there are not yet many additional long-term decision layers in the battle loop itself

5. Defeat recovery is functional but not dramatic
- replayability improves when loss is readable and recoverable
- current defeat flow still needs a stronger visual reset cycle


15. HOW TO OPTIMIZE THE GAME LOOP FOR REPLAYABILITY
---------------------------------------------------

Best next improvements, based on the current version:

1. Make death states fully authoritative visually
- when manager HP reaches 0:
  - explicitly trigger Character death state
  - wait for death presentation
  - then respawn/reset

Why:
- this makes victories and defeats feel meaningful

2. Normalize high-wave combat scaling
- keep wave growth
- but smooth the curve so upgrades and enemy scaling stay competitive longer

Why:
- replayability dies when progression feels impossible rather than challenging

3. Strengthen reward milestones
- add stronger differences at:
  - boss clears
  - milestone waves
  - upgrade breakpoints

Why:
- replayability improves when players can feel "I reached something important"

4. Add more variation in enemy identity
- enemy pattern strings already exist:
  - `measured`
  - `aggressive`
  - `feint`
- they are not yet strongly expressed in the visual combat layer

Why:
- replayability improves when later waves feel behaviorally different, not just numerically larger

5. Make defeat feed progression more clearly
- loss should still feel useful
- for example:
  - clearer gold gain loop
  - stronger upgrade return
  - more visible "come back stronger" rhythm

Why:
- idle replayability depends on loss still contributing to future success

6. Improve the mid-loop decisions outside battle
- hero slots
- gacha
- formation/roster bonuses
- upgrade tradeoffs

Why:
- the battle loop becomes more replayable when preparation choices affect the outcome
